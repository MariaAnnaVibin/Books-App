Alice in Wonderland - Lewis Carroll

Alice's Adventures in Wonderland by Lewis Carroll is a whimsical tale about a young girl named Alice who falls down a rabbit hole into a fantastical world filled with eccentric characters and bizarre adventures. Throughout her journey, Alice encounters strange creatures like the Cheshire Cat, the Mad Hatter, and the Queen of Hearts, all while questioning reality and her own identity. The story explores themes of curiosity, imagination, and the absurdity of rules and authority.

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone - J.K.Rowling

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone follows a young boy, Harry Potter, who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard. He is introduced to the magical world and attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he uncovers the truth about his parents' mysterious death. As Harry navigates his new life, he forms friendships and faces challenges, ultimately confronting a dark secret involving the powerful Sorcerer's Stone.

Black Beauty -Anna Sewell

Black Beauty by Anna Sewell is a timeless classic narrated from the perspective of a horse, chronicling his life experiences from a carefree colt to a hard-working animal in Victorian England. The novel highlights themes of kindness, compassion, and the importance of humane treatment of animals, offering a powerful commentary on the treatment of horses during that era. Through Black Beauty's journey, readers are given insights into the bond between humans and animals, and the impact of cruelty and kindness.

Charlie and the Chocolate Factory - Roald Dahl

Charlie and the Chocolate Factory by Roald Dahl is a beloved children's novel that follows the adventures of a poor boy named Charlie Bucket as he wins a golden ticket to tour Willy Wonka's magical chocolate factory. Along with other ticket winners, Charlie experiences the whimsical and sometimes perilous creations inside the factory, where the misbehavior of the other children leads to comical consequences. Ultimately, Charlie's kindness and humility are rewarded as he is chosen to inherit the factory, changing his life forever.

The Blue Umbrella -Ruskin Bond

The Blue Umbrella by Ruskin Bond is a heartwarming tale set in a small Himalayan village. It follows the story of a young girl named Binya, who trades her leopard claw necklace for a beautiful blue umbrella, sparking envy among the villagers, especially a shopkeeper named Ram Bharosa. The story highlights themes of kindness, contentment, and the simple joys of life, all wrapped in Bond's charming, descriptive style.
